# Editorial Notes for Future Impact Updates

These notes should guide future Impact Notes, public impact pages, and partnership CTAs on `impact.urduai.org`.

## Urdu Ai Brand Treatment

- When `Urdu Ai` appears as the brand on a dark, blue, or visual brand surface, style it as a brand mark: `Urdu` in white and `Ai` in Urdu Ai yellow.
- Use the existing inline pattern for dark surfaces:
  - `Urdu`: `var(--on-primary)`
  - `Ai`: `#f4d644`
  - class pattern: `.brand-inline.on-dark`
- Do not force this color treatment into normal article body copy on white backgrounds. In paragraphs, captions, metadata, links, and article text on light backgrounds, `Urdu Ai` can remain readable plain text.
- Use the colored treatment selectively for brand-led CTAs, hero brand moments, dark cards, and places where the name is functioning as an identity mark rather than a normal phrase.

## Impact Notes Editorial Standard

- Keep Impact Notes public-facing. Avoid internal phrases such as "built for discovery," "answer engines," "SEO content," or operational notes that sound like they are written for staff.
- Write for partners, supporters, educators, AI access programs, institutions, and community readers.
- Keep the voice clear, practical, and grounded in real field work.
- Explain public numbers carefully. Use "reported learner records" or "training completions" unless a number is verified as unique beneficiaries.
- Do not publish Phase 3 internal material.
- Present women participation as `51%` on the public site. Do not use raw female learner counts in headline metrics.

## SEO and AEO Checklist

Every new Impact Note should include:

- A focused slug with natural keywords.
- One clear H1.
- A search title around 50-65 characters when possible.
- A meta description around 130-160 characters.
- A visible byline, such as `By Urdu Ai Impact Team`.
- A visible trust signal when relevant, such as `Reviewed by WANG`.
- A short answer section near the top for answer engines and AI summaries.
- Practical H2 headings that answer real search questions.
- FAQ content, rendered visibly and included in FAQ schema.
- Article schema, FAQ schema, and breadcrumb schema.
- Source or recognition links when claims mention partners, funders, public recognition, or institutional relationships.
- A sitemap update with the new article URL and current `lastmod` date.
- Descriptive image alt text.
- WebP image variants where practical, with JPG fallback.

## Partner and Recognition Language

- When mentioning the AI Opportunity Fund, use the public partnership line carefully:
  - `AI Opportunity Fund: Asia-Pacific, implemented by WANG in collaboration with AVPN and supported by Google.org and the Asian Development Bank.`
- Link to AVPN, WANG, Google.org, and ADB when the article uses this recognition for credibility.
- Do not overstate funder involvement beyond the public wording.

## Article CTA Pattern

- Keep CTAs action-oriented and public:
  - `Discuss partnership`
  - `View institutional MoUs`
  - `Request impact brief`
- On dark CTA cards, apply the branded inline treatment to `Urdu Ai`.
- Keep CTA copy short and practical. Avoid decorative language.
